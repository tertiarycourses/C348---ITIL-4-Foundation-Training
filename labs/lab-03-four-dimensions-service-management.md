# Lab 03 - Four Dimensions of Service Management

## Objectives

- Explain the four dimensions of service management.
- Assess a service using all four dimensions.
- Identify risks caused by ignoring a dimension.
- Create a balanced service improvement checklist.

## Scenario

BrightDesk has improved its ticket workflow, but user satisfaction is still low. The team has focused heavily on tools and ignored people, suppliers, and processes.

## Steps

### 1. List the Four Dimensions

Write:

```text
Organizations and people
Information and technology
Partners and suppliers
Value streams and processes
```

### 2. Assess the Service

Create a table:

| Dimension | Current Issue | Improvement Idea |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organizations and people | Agents lack knowledge articles | Train agents and improve knowledge base |

Complete all four dimensions.

### 3. Identify External Factors

Consider:

- Political.
- Economic.
- Social.
- Technological.
- Legal.
- Environmental.

Write one factor that could affect BrightDesk.

### 4. Build a Balanced Checklist

Before approving a service improvement, ask:

1. Do people understand the change?
2. Is information accurate and accessible?
3. Are tools fit for purpose?
4. Are suppliers aligned?
5. Is the value stream clear?
6. Are processes simple and practical?

### 5. Apply the Checklist

Apply the checklist to a new self-service password reset service.

## Validation

You should have a four-dimension assessment, external factor notes, and a balanced checklist.

## Checkpoint Questions

1. Why is technology alone insufficient?
2. What happens if supplier dependencies are ignored?
3. How do value streams differ from isolated processes?
4. Which dimension includes culture and competencies?

## Exam Focus

The exam may ask which dimension is most relevant in a scenario. Look for clues about people, technology, suppliers, or workflows.
