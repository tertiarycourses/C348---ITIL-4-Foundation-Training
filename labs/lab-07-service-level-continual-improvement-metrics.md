# Lab 07 - Service Level Management, Continual Improvement, Metrics

## Objectives

- Explain service level management.
- Define service level targets.
- Build a continual improvement register.
- Apply the continual improvement model.
- Choose useful service metrics.

## Scenario

BrightDesk users say support is slow, but IT only reports the number of tickets closed. Management asks for better service levels and improvement priorities.

## Steps

### 1. Define Service Level Targets

Create targets:

| Service | Target | Measurement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Priority 1 incident response | 15 minutes | Time from logging to response |
| Standard laptop request | 3 business days | Request open to fulfilled |

Add at least three targets.

### 2. Identify Experience Metrics

Include:

- Customer satisfaction.
- First contact resolution.
- Reopened tickets.
- SLA achievement.
- Backlog age.
- Major incident count.

### 3. Build an Improvement Register

Use:

| Improvement | Value | Owner | Priority | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Improve password reset self-service | Reduce service desk contacts | Service desk lead | High | Proposed |

### 4. Apply the Continual Improvement Model

Answer:

```text
What is the vision?
Where are we now?
Where do we want to be?
How do we get there?
Take action.
Did we get there?
How do we keep momentum going?
```

### 5. Avoid Bad Metrics

Identify one metric that could drive poor behavior. Explain how to balance it with another metric.

Example: closing tickets quickly may reduce quality unless balanced with customer satisfaction and reopened ticket rate.

## Validation

You should have service level targets, metrics, improvement register, and continual improvement model answers.

## Checkpoint Questions

1. What is the purpose of service level management?
2. Why should metrics support value?
3. What is an improvement register?
4. Why can a single metric be dangerous?

## Exam Focus

Continual improvement questions often ask which step of the model applies or how improvement should be managed systematically.
